## 07 — Etymology Card Copy (Physical Insert, Folded Card)

**Front**

> Peace was always bilingual.

**Inside Left — Chinese side**

> 和平
> hé píng
>
> 和 — grain (禾) beside a mouth (口).
> The image of speaking with enough.
> 平 — a level surface, still water, equilibrium.
>
> Together: the condition of a world with enough and in balance.

**Inside Right — Hebrew side**

> שלום
> shalom
>
> From the root שׁלם (sh-l-m):
> wholeness, completeness, nothing missing.
>
> Not the absence of conflict —
> the presence of everything that should be there.

**Back**

> This pendant was designed by finding
> the visual space where both scripts
> could inhabit the same line.
>
> It took longer than expected.
> That felt right.
>
